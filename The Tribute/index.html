Abdul Qadeer Khan

1936-2021

Mohsin-e-Pakistan

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, a visionary scientist and a pillar of Pakistan's scientific community, devoted his life to advancing his nation's technological prowess and sovereignty. As the architect of Pakistan's nuclear program, his relentless pursuit of knowledge and innovation transformed his country into a formidable force in the global arena. His contributions extend beyond the realms of science, embodying a spirit of resilience and patriotism that inspires future generations. Dr. Khan's legacy is not only etched in the annals of history but also in the hearts of those who cherish the values of dedication, excellence, and national pride.

• Born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, British India.

• Moved to Pakistan after the 1947 partition.

• Earned a degree in metallurgical engineering from the University of Berlin and a PhD from Katholieke,

• Founded Khan Research Laboratories (KRL) in 1976.

• Played a key role in developing Pakistan’s nuclear weapons program.

• Pakistan conducted its first successful nuclear tests in 1998 under his leadership.

• Faced controversies and international sanctions due to nuclear proliferation activities.

• Passed away on October 10, 2021.
